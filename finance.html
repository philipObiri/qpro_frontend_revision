The Eight-Week Professional Development Certificate in Finance is an intensive full-time program designed to provide a comprehensive understanding of finance. It focuses on vital areas such as financial analysis, management, corporate finance, investments, and financial markets, which are crucial for a thriving career in the financial industry.

Aim:
  • To equip students with a profound knowledge of finance principles and their application in various business contexts

Objectives:
  • Develop a solid understanding of finance principles and their practical business applications.
  • Gain expertise in key finance areas including financial analysis, management, corporate finance, and investments.
  • Enhance the ability to make strategic financial decisions in the business sector.

Upon completing the program, students will::

  • Possess proficiency in analyzing financial statements for informed decision-making.
  • Have an in-depth understanding of financial management and corporate finance.
  • Acquire skills in investment analysis and comprehension of financial market dynamics.
  • Develop strategies for effective financial risk management.

Course Outline

Week 1: Introduction to Finance
  • Fundamental principles of financial management, financial markets, and instruments.

Week 2 - Week 3: Foundations of Financial Analysis
  • Methods in financial statement analysis, forecasting, and portfolio management basics

Week 4: Financial Management in Organizations
  • Core concepts in organizational financial management, budgeting, and capital structure.

Week 5 - Week 6: Corporate Finance: Principles and Practices
  • Comprehensive study of corporate finance, including capital budgeting, financing decisions, and valuation

Week 7: Introduction to Investments and Financial Markets
  • Basic investment theories, asset pricing models, and an overview of global financial markets.

Week 8: Foundations of Financial Markets and Instruments
  • Understanding various financial instruments and the structure of financial markets.

Assessment Methods:
  • Continuous assessments through quizzes, assignments, and case studies.
  • Practical financial projects to apply learned concepts.
  • A capstone project that integrates knowledge from all course modules.
  • A final examination to assess overall understanding.

Learning Methods:
  • Engaging online lectures that are compact and interactive.
  • Case-based learning for applying theory to real-world financial scenarios.
  • Collaborative projects, peer reviews, and access to digital resources.
  • Utilization of financial simulation tools for hands-on learning.
